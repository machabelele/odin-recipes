How to make boiled chicken

Ingredients

  • 2–3 chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • Water (enough to cover the chicken)

Steps

  1. Prep the chicken: Rinse 2–3 chicken breasts or thighs, pat dry, and season with salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs (like thyme or rosemary).
  2. Boil the water: Fill a pot with enough water to cover the chicken, add 1 chopped onion, 2 garlic cloves, and a bay leaf, then bring to a boil.
  3. Cook the chicken: Add chicken to boiling water, reduce to a simmer, and cook for 15–20 minutes until fully cooked.
  4. Check doneness: Ensure internal temperature reaches 75°C (165°F) or cut into the thickest part—juices should run clear.
  5. Serve: Remove chicken, let rest for a few minutes, slice or shred, and use in salads, sandwiches, or soups.
